The Problem

Your product runs on layered systems: services written across eras, databases stretched by growth, caches tuned to protect fragile dependencies, dashboards watching for the next spike.

Code review only sees the diff. Great architects embed in your reality; commodity AI reviewers don't. They respond to the diff in front of them, unaware of production hot paths, live traffic patterns, or the brittle dependencies your team cares about.

The failures are born in runtime reality. Latency regressions appear when retries slam a saturated service, migrations stall because replicas lag, and a ‚Äúsimple‚Äù query turns catastrophic on 80 million rows. Those risks only show up when you understand how the system behaves today.

System-Aware Reviews

With visibility into your production telemetry (APM traces, slow query dashboards, and live traffic patterns) Ravi enforces risk checks before merge. That N+1 query might handle 10 req/min fine, but at 500 req/min it becomes an outage waiting to happen.

True Peer, Not a Linter

Other tools give the same advice to a 1,000-user startup as they would to Google. Ravi enforces trade-offs tuned to the risk profile you run today—accelerating when the blast radius is low, hardening when production impact is high.

Built for Engineering Leaders

You can't hire a senior architect for every team, but you can scale production risk guardrails across your organization. Ravi ensures every PR gets context-aware enforcement so issues are handled before they reach customers.
